Frequently Asked Questions

I'm a landlord. How fast can I get a dumpster for a unit turnover?

We understand that time is money. We prioritize calls from property managers and can often provide same day dumpster rental or, at the latest, next-day service. Our goal is to help you get your unit rent-ready as quickly as possible.

I'm handling an estate. What if the cleanout takes longer than the standard rental period?

We get it. These jobs can be unpredictable. We offer flexible, affordable rental extensions. Just communicate with us. We’re not going to pull the dumpster out from under you when you’re in the middle of a difficult job.

What if my tenant left hazardous materials like paint or chemicals?

You are responsible for what goes into the dumpster. Unfortunately, we cannot accept hazardous materials. They must be disposed of separately according to your local regulations. The dumpster is for the furniture, trash, and general debris.

My property was damaged in a storm. Can I put tree branches and shingles in the same dumpster?

Yes. In an emergency cleanup, you can mix natural debris like wood and branches with construction debris like roofing, siding, and drywall.

How does payment work for an estate? Can the bill be paid out of the estate's funds later?

Our policy is typically payment upfront via credit card when you place the order. This secures the rental. We then provide a clear invoice that can be submitted to the estate for reimbursement.

King City, OR Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

King City is a city in Washington County, Oregon, United States. Its name was picked arbitrarily by the Tualatin Development Company, Inc., which used a royalty theme in naming the city streets. The population was 3,111 at the 2010 census. Fire protection and EMS services are provided through Tualatin Valley Fire and Rescue.
